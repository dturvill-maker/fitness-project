TRAINING PRINCIPLES

The Foundation of Physical Progress

Forget programs that promise miracles. Real progress comes from mastering the basics and applying them consistently over time.

  1. Progressive Overload
    • Add weight, reps, or sets each week
    • Track every workout in a notebook or app
    • Deload every 4–6 weeks to recover
  2. Compound Movements First
    • Squat, deadlift, bench, overhead press, row
    • These recruit the most muscle and build the most strength
    • Isolation work comes after — not instead of
  3. Consistency Over Intensity
    • 3 average workouts per week beats 1 perfect one
    • Show up even when motivation is low
    • Discipline is a skill — train it like a muscle

NUTRITION

Fuel Like You Mean It

You can't out-train a bad diet. Nutrition is 60% of the result.

🥩

Protein First

Hit 0.8–1g per pound of bodyweight daily. Prioritize chicken, beef, eggs, Greek yogurt, and cottage cheese. Protein preserves muscle in a deficit and builds it in a surplus.

⚖️

Calories Are King

Track your intake for at least 2 weeks. You can't manage what you don't measure. A modest 300–500 calorie deficit or surplus will get you where you want to go without extremes.

💧

Hydration Matters

Half your bodyweight in ounces, minimum. Dehydration tanks performance by up to 20%. Add electrolytes during heavy training days. Water is the cheapest performance supplement.

😴

Sleep is Non-Negotiable

7-9 hours of sleep is when your muscles actually grow. Training breaks muscle down — sleep and nutrition builds it back up. No supplement replaces a good night's rest.

MINDSET

The Mental Game

01
Identity Over Goals

Don't say "I want to lose 20 lbs." Say "I am someone who trains consistently and eats well." Identity drives behavior.

02
Embrace the Process

Progress is slow and non-linear. The plateau is where most people quit — it's where you must push hardest.

03
Environment Design

Pack your gym bag the night before. Keep protein-rich food visible. Remove friction from good habits, add friction to bad ones.
